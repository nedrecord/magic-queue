Summon FAQ

Answers to common questions about using Summon at the table.

How Summon Works

+ What is Summon?

A simple table request system for magicians. People scan a code and you receive their table number in your queue.

+ Do people need to install anything?

No. A phone camera and browser are all that’s required.

+ Does Summon require an internet connection?

Yes. Summon is web based. Your device needs WiFi or cellular data for requests to appear.

+ What if someone has weak signal?

If they can load the summon page once, the message displays. They only need enough service to reach that page.

+ Can Summon be used offline?

No. Real-time requests require an active connection.

QR Codes and Table Requests

+ How many tables can Summon handle?

Up to 50. Each table has a unique QR code.

+ What happens when someone scans the QR?

Their table number enters your queue, timestamped and sorted oldest to newest.

+ What happens if someone repeatedly scans the same QR code?

Only that table’s timestamp updates. A spammer would effectively move themselves down the queue as newer tables come in.

+ What does “Pause Queue” do?

It shows a short “brief pause” message instead of the normal confirmation. They are still added to your queue

+ How do I clear a table after visiting them?

Tap “Done” next to that table in the queue.

+ Can multiple magicians use Summon in the same venue?

Yes, as long as each magician has their own account. They’ll need to coordinate their own table numbering.

QR Codes, Printing, and the Placard Generator

+ What are the QR codes for?

They route table requests into your queue.

+ Can I re-download my QR codes later?

No. Summon does not store your QR images. If you lose them, you must generate new ones.

+ What is the Placard Generator?

Many performers aren’t design-savvy, so Summon includes a simple tool that creates clean, printable table cards from your QR ZIP.

+ I prefer to design my own signage. Is that allowed?

Of course. The Placard Generator is optional.

+ What if someone scans one of my signs outside the venue?

It still triggers a table request. You should generally collect your placards after the gig unless the venue prints and manages their own signage.

Subscription and Billing

+ How do I manage my subscription?

The Account page shows your email, subscription status, and renewal details.

+ What happens when my subscription expires?

Your account becomes “Not subscribed” and the queue will not accept new table requests until you renew.

+ Can Summon be used by a venue that hires different magicians?

Yes, but each magician still needs their own individual account. Summon is currently structured around performer accounts rather than venue-wide logins.

Data and Privacy

+ Does Summon sell or share data?

No. Only minimal operational data is stored, and none is sold or shared.
